Melodic structures: the Implication-Realization model
     The IR model predicts these melodic principles (from Krumhansl, 1995):
Registral Direction – small implication intervals are followed by realization intervals in the same direction; large implication intervals are followed by realization intervals in the opposite direction
Intervallic Difference – small intervals imply similar-sized intervals; large intervals imply smaller intervals
Registral Return – intervals imply similarly sized intervals in the opposite direction  (image source: Krumhansl, 1995)
Proximity – realized intervals should be no larger than a perfect 4th.
Closure – realized intervals tend to smaller and in the opposite direction than implied intervals.

Gestalt principles
The Gestalt principles are:
1. Similarity - elements that are more similar (i.e. share common features) are more likely to be grouped together.
(image source: http://www.usask.ca/education/coursework/skaalid/theory/gestalt/gestaltimages/) We perceive the white dots as one group and the pink dots as another group; this illustrates grouping by similarity of colour.
2. Symmetry - symmetrical figures are perceived as being grouped together.
 The pair of shapes on the left is more likely to be perceived as a group compared to the pair on the right; this is because the left pair is symmetrical around the dotted line whereas the right pair is asymmetrical.
3. Proximity - elements that are close together are more likely to be grouped together.
 (image source: http://www.usask.ca/education/coursework/skaalid/theory/gestalt/gestaltimages/)
The dots that are closer together are perceived as being grouped together.
4. Good continuation - we tend to perceive figures as being continuous.
 The red diagonal is perceived as one line instead of two, due to the principle of good continuation.
5. Closure - we tend to see complete figures despite missing parts.
6. Common fate - elements that change similarly over time tend to be grouped together. 
 These two pandas are grouped together because they move together.
